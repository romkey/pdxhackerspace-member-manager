Admin FAQ

Quick reference for stewards using Member Manager. Details live in Settings, runbooks, and your org’s own policies.

Where do I change member-facing wording?

Open Settings → Text Fragments. Each fragment has a stable key (for example help_faq, navbar_help, member_help). Edit HTML in the admin UI, or set a Source URL (including raw GitHub file URLs—the sync normalizes blob links) and use Sync from URL on one fragment or Sync all from URLs for everything configured.

How do the Help pages map to fragments?

Help → General uses help_general, falling back to navbar_help if general is empty. Help → FAQ uses help_faq. Help → Admin FAQ uses help_admin_faq and is only shown to admins.

How do I invite or onboard a new member?

Use Invitations to send a link that creates their account, or align accounts with your identity provider (for example Authentik) using the sync actions on a user. Exact onboarding rules are up to your org.

What is impersonation, and when should I use it?

Admins can impersonate a member to reproduce what they see. Use sparingly, for support, and stop impersonation when finished. Follow your org’s privacy and access policy.

How are membership applications handled?

Configurable application forms, verification gates, and review live under Applications and related settings. Train reviewers on your pipeline: pending → approved/rejected, and any notifications you use.

How do payments show up?

Integrations such as PayPal, Recharge, and Ko-Fi can sync into the app; you can also import or link payments manually where supported. Reconcile regularly and correct member linkage when something lands on the wrong record.

What is the Access section for?

Access controllers and RFID readers tie physical credentials to members. Scripts or jobs push allowed keys to door hardware—coordinate with whoever maintains controllers and the network path to them.

How does training data work?

Define Training topics in Settings, then record who trained whom on member profiles. Use this to enforce equipment rules and audits.

What are incoming webhooks?

Incoming webhooks receive events from external systems at stable URLs. Configure slugs and secrets carefully; test in staging when available.

How do Authentik and Slack fit in?

Authentik (or another OIDC provider) handles login and often group membership sync. Slack integration is typically for notifications—see environment variables and service objects for your deployment.

Where do background jobs run?

Sidekiq processes queues; admins with the link can open the Sidekiq Web UI from the nav. Failed jobs and retries usually need Redis and worker logs.

What should I check before a big event or renewal push?

Confirm payment sync is healthy, fragments and emails read correctly, RFIDs enroll for new members, and someone knows how to deactivate accounts or badges for people who leave.
